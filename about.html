Hi, I'm Tyler!

I'm an Arizonan game developer who primarily works on the design aspects of games, though I am open to expanding past games! I've always been interested in the visual aspects of games. It started with drawing the various characters from Pokemon, but my infautations later expanded to Minecraft, Fire Emblem, and League of Legends. To me, games are essentially a story with interactive elements, which is an integral way for the developers to connect with the target audience. Now, I find myself analyzing these visual aspects I come across in games, along with the more technical ones to figure out what leaves an impact, and what creates a good, enjoyable experience for the end user. During my free time, I am usually trying to learn, because I believe that knowledge is power, and I want to get better at what I do.

Education

I earned my bachelors degree in Graphic Information Technology from Arizona State University, where I learned about Animation, 3D modelling, and Design. Additionally, I learned a lot about game development and the Unity engine while pursuing a game development certificate. I've worked with multiple groups of people during my classes, working on quick game jams and larger, more comprehensive indie games. My time at ASU also included employment as a teachers assistant and grader, where I helped my peers improve by providing assistance and feedback.

What do I do?

I would currently consider myself more of a generalist, as I am able to understand and work with 3D modelling, animation, texturing, design, illustration and print. I can work with many common design tools such as the Adobe Suite, and Figma, along with 3D modelling software like Autodesk Maya and Blender. Also, I have experience working within Unity and coding C# scripts for Unity games. My hope for the future is to find something to specialize in, and my current aspiration is to get more into UI/UX research and design, because I am interested in how people interact with products.

My favorite projects

Iris 3D model

Iris is a 3D model that I created, straight from a concept. Created as a senior capstone, I modelled and textured by hand, and set the model up with a basic rig.

Animal Crossing Concept

A demonstration of user flows and interfaces with a conceptual Animal Crossing App. It guides the user through daily tasks in Animal Crossing, while also serving as a sort of wiki for players.

Sword and Shield short

A short trailer with the purpose of announcing a new game. My goal with this project was to create a 3D composition and test lighting setups within Maya.

Cheesecake Distribution

A game where you create cheesecakes for hungry mice. I worked with a few friends to build this game within Unity. My focus was mostly asset and scene building.
